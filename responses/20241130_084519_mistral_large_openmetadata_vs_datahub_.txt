OpenMetadata and DataHub are both open-source platforms designed for metadata management and data governance, but they have different focuses and features.

OpenMetadata:

1. **Focus**: OpenMetadata is focused on providing a unified metadata store, data discovery, data lineage, and data quality. It aims to simplify data governance and metadata management.

2. **Architecture**: It uses a modular, pluggable architecture that allows it to integrate with various data sources, data catalogs, and data quality tools. It also supports federated search across multiple data catalogs.

3. **Data Lineage**: OpenMetadata provides detailed data lineage, tracking data flow from source to destination, including transformations and joins.

4. **Data Quality**: It integrates with data quality tools to provide visibility into data quality issues and their impact on data pipelines.

5. **Open Standards**: OpenMetadata is built on open standards and protocols, making it highly interoperable with other tools and platforms.

6. **Community and Support**: OpenMetadata has a growing community and is backed by the OpenMetadata Foundation. It also offers commercial support through OpenMetadata, Inc.

DataHub:

1. **Focus**: DataHub, developed by LinkedIn, is a metadata search and discovery tool that focuses on making data easily discoverable and understandable.

2. **Architecture**: DataHub is built on a pluggable architecture that allows it to integrate with various data sources and tools. It uses a graph database to store and query metadata.

3. **Data Lineage**: DataHub provides data lineage, but it's not as detailed as OpenMetadata. It focuses more on the relationships between datasets and less on the transformations and joins.

4. **Data Quality**: DataHub does not have built-in data quality features, but it can integrate with external data quality tools.

5. **Open Standards**: DataHub also uses open standards and protocols, making it interoperable with other tools and platforms.

6. **Community and Support**: DataHub has a large community and is backed by LinkedIn. It also offers commercial support through DataHub, Inc.

In summary, OpenMetadata provides more detailed data lineage and built-in data quality features, while DataHub focuses more on data discovery and understandability. Both platforms are open-source and have growing communities and commercial support options. The choice between the two will depend on your specific needs and use cases.
