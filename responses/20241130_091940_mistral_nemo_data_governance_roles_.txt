Data governance involves a set of roles that work together to ensure data is accurate, consistent, secure, and compliant with relevant regulations. Here are some key roles in data governance:

1. **Data Governance Steering Committee (DGSC)**:
   - Provides strategic direction and oversight for data governance.
   - Consists of senior leaders from various departments, including IT, business units, and compliance.
   - Ensures data governance aligns with business objectives and regulatory requirements.

2. **Data Governance Manager/Chief Data Officer (CDO)**:
   - Oversees the day-to-day operations of data governance.
   - Ensures data quality, security, and compliance.
   - Collaborates with stakeholders to define data strategy, policies, and standards.
   - Responsible for data governance budget, resources, and team management.

3. **Data Stewards**:
   - Subject matter experts who oversee specific data domains or systems.
   - Ensure data accuracy, completeness, and consistency within their assigned areas.
   - Collaborate with data users to understand data needs and provide data-related guidance.
   - Monitor and enforce data policies and standards.

4. **Data Architects**:
   - Design and maintain the overall data architecture, including data models, databases, and data flows.
   - Ensure data consistency and integration across systems.
   - Collaborate with data stewards to define data standards and metadata.

5. **Data Analysts/Scientists**:
   - Extract, transform, and load (ETL) data for analysis.
   - Clean and prepare data for analysis.
   - Perform statistical analysis and create data visualizations and reports.
   - Collaborate with data users to understand data needs and provide insights.

6. **Data Engineers**:
   - Develop and maintain data pipelines and infrastructure.
   - Ensure data is securely stored, processed, and transferred.
   - Collaborate with data architects to design and implement data infrastructure.

7. **Data Quality Analysts**:
   - Monitor and assess data quality.
   - Identify and address data issues, such as inaccuracies, inconsistencies, or missing values.
   - Collaborate with data stewards to improve data quality.

8. **Data Privacy Officer/Chief Privacy Officer (CPO)**:
   - Ensures data privacy and compliance with relevant regulations, such as GDPR, CCPA, or HIPAA.
   - Develops and maintains data privacy policies and procedures.
   - Collaborates with data governance to integrate privacy into data management processes.

9. **Data Protection Officer (DPO)**:
   - Ensures data security and protection.
   - Monitors and manages data security risks.
   - Collaborates with IT and data governance to implement security measures and respond to security incidents.

10. **Data Users**:
    - End-users who access and use data for decision-making, analysis, or other purposes.
    - Collaborate with data governance to provide feedback on data needs and usage.

These roles may vary depending on the organization's size, industry, and data maturity. However, they provide a comprehensive overview of the key roles involved in data governance.
