A Data Mesh is a decentralized data architecture that emphasizes domain-oriented, self-service data management. It was first introduced by Zhamak Dehghani in 2019. The core principles of a Data Mesh are:

1. **Domain-oriented**: Data is organized around business domains or subdomains, rather than being centralized in a single repository.
2. **Self-service**: Data consumers can access and work with data without relying on centralized teams or bottlenecks.
3. **Federated governance**: Data governance is distributed across the organization, with each domain or team responsible for their own data.
4. **Data as a product**: Data is treated as a product that is maintained, updated, and delivered to consumers.
