Data governance encompasses a range of roles and responsibilities related to the management and utilization of data within an organization. The specific roles can vary depending on the size of the organization, industry, and regulatory requirements, but here are some key roles typically found within a data governance framework:

1. **Chief Data Officer (CDO)**:
   - Responsible for the overall data strategy and governance within the organization.
   - Ensures data is leveraged as a strategic asset.
   - Typically interfaces with executive leadership.

2. **Data Governance Council**:
   - A cross-functional group that provides oversight and direction for data governance initiatives.
   - Includes stakeholders from various departments such as IT, legal, compliance, and business units.

3. **Data Steward**:
   - Responsible for managing and overseeing data assets within a specific domain.
   - Ensures data quality, integrity, and consistency.
   - Acts as a liaison between IT and business users.

4. **Data Owner**:
   - The individual or group responsible for a specific data set, including its security and usage.
   - Makes decisions about data access and usage.

5. **Data Architect**:
   - Designs and maintains the organization’s data architecture, ensuring it aligns with business goals.
   - Works on data modeling, database design, and system integration.

6. **Data Quality Manager**:
   - Focuses on ensuring the accuracy, reliability, and timeliness of data.
   - Implements processes for data quality monitoring and improvement.

7. **Data Analyst**:
   - Analyzes data to derive insights that can help improve business decisions.
   - Works closely with data stewards and architects to ensure that data is fit for purpose.

8. **Compliance Officer**:
   - Ensures that the organization’s data practices comply with relevant laws and regulations (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA).
   - Works with data governance teams to identify and mitigate risks.

9. **Business Unit Representatives**:
   - Representatives from various business units who advocate for their data needs and contribute to governance policies and practices.
   - Helps align data management with business objectives.

10. **IT Data Governance Lead**:
    - Oversees the technical aspects of data governance initiatives.
    - Coordinates with the IT department to implement data governance policies and tools.

11. **Metadata Manager**:
    - Responsible for managing data metadata, which includes data definitions, data lineage, and cataloging.
    - Facilitates better data understanding and usage across the organization.

12. **Data Privacy Officer**:
    - Focuses on ensuring that the organization's data practices respect user privacy.
    - Works closely with legal and compliance teams to address privacy concerns.

Each of these roles contributes to the overarching goal of effective data governance, which is to ensure that an organization manages its data assets in a way that supports strategic objectives while maintaining compliance and minimizing risks.
