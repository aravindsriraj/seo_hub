1.  **Roles**: Snowflake uses a role-based access control system. Roles are collections of privileges that are assigned to a user or another role. This simplifies granting permissions and also allows you to manage large organizations through fewer role management objects. Users or other roles can be assigned to a role, inheriting all the privileges associated with that role.

2.  **Grants**: Grants are used to assign specific permissions to roles or users. There are two types of grants: own and WITH GRANT OPTION. Own grants are typically used for managing the creation and dropping of database objects and have no implications. The WITH GRANT OPTION is used for granting permissions to other users or roles to further distribute privilege to others.

3.  **Privileges**: These control what operations users can perform on objects in Snowflake such as databases, schemas, tables, views, stages, mappers, virtual warehouses, and warehouses. The privileges include SELECT, INSERT, UPDATE, DELETE, DROP, CREATE, etc.
