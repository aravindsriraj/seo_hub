The pricing of data catalog solutions varies significantly based on the vendor, deployment model (cloud or on-premises), user count, data volume, features, and enterprise size. Below is a general guide to how data catalog pricing is structured, though exact pricing details often require contacting vendors directly.

---

### 1. **Pricing Models**
Data catalog vendors typically use one or more of the following pricing models:

- **Subscription-Based Pricing**: 
  - Most cloud-based data catalogs follow a subscription model, often billed monthly or annually.
  - Pricing may depend on the number of users, data sources, or the volume of data.

- **Per User Pricing**:
  - Vendors may charge per user (e.g., per analyst or data steward) accessing the platform.
  - Common for organizations with small or medium-sized teams.

- **Tiered Pricing**:
  - Features are grouped into packages (e.g., Basic, Professional, Enterprise), with increasing capabilities and pricing for each tier.

- **Custom Pricing**:
  - Enterprise-level customers may require custom solutions, where pricing depends on the organization’s specific needs, including scale and integrations.

- **Usage-Based Pricing**:
  - Some vendors charge based on the number of data assets, API calls, or data volume scanned and cataloged.

---

### 2. **Sample Vendor Pricing Ranges**
Below are typical pricing patterns for some leading data catalog platforms. Note that vendors often provide only limited pricing information publicly, requiring you to contact sales for detailed quotes.

#### **Cloud-Based Data Catalog Solutions**
- **Alation**

- **Collibra**

- **Informatica Enterprise Data Catalog**
  - Pricing: Custom (not publicly disclosed).
  - Typically targeted at large organizations and offered as part of Informatica’s broader suite of data management tools.

- **Google Cloud Data Catalog**
  - Pricing: **Free (currently)** for metadata management with limited features, and additional costs if combined with other Google Cloud services.

#### **Open Source (or Freemium) Options**

  - Cost: Free (open source).
  - No licensing cost, but operational costs may include cloud hosting or on-premises infrastructure.

- **Amundsen (by Lyft)**
  - Cost: Free (open source).
  - Development and maintenance handled in-house by organizations.

### 3. **Common Pricing Influencers**
Several factors influence the final cost of a data catalog solution:

- **Number of Users**:
  - Many vendors price solutions based on active users or assigned roles (e.g., analysts, data stewards, administrators).

- **Number of Data Sources/Assets**:
  - Larger organizations handling vast datasets or connecting to many data sources may face higher pricing tiers.

- **Deployment Model**:
  - On-premises deployments often have higher upfront costs for infrastructure and maintenance compared to subscription-based cloud solutions.

- **Integration Capabilities**:
  - Some vendors charge extra for connectors to third-party systems, such as BI tools, data lakes, or cloud warehouses.

- **Advanced Features**:
  - Features like machine learning-based data classification, lineage visualization, and policy management may be available at higher pricing tiers.

---

### 4. **Estimating Costs**
- **SMBs (Small to Medium Businesses)**: 
  Can expect subscription fees between **$12,000–$50,000 annually** depending on complexity and size.

- **Enterprises**:
  Large organizations with extensive needs may pay **$100,000–$500,000+ annually**, especially for custom solutions that include integrations, support, and governance tools.
