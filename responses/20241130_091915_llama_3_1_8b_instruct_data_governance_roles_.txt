Data governance is a critical component of any organization's data management strategy, and various roles play a crucial part in ensuring its success. Here are some common data governance roles:

1. **Data Governance Officer/Manager**: Responsible for leading the data governance program, setting policies, and overseeing its implementation.
2. **Data Steward**: Responsible for ensuring the quality, accuracy, and security of specific data assets within their domain. They often act as a liaison between business stakeholders and the data governance team.
3. **Data Custodian**: Responsible for maintaining the data assets, including data creation, storage, and updating. They ensure data is accurate, complete, and up-to-date.
4. **Metadata Manager**: Responsible for managing metadata, including data definitions, business rules, and data quality metrics.
5. **Data Quality Analyst**: Responsible for monitoring and improving data quality, including data profiling, data validation, and data cleansing.
6. **Data Security Officer**: Responsible for ensuring the security and integrity of data, including access control, data encryption, and data backup.
7. **Compliance Officer**: Responsible for ensuring data governance policies align with regulatory requirements, such as GDPR, HIPAA, or CCPA.
8. **Business Analyst**: Works with stakeholders to understand business requirements and ensure data governance policies meet those needs.
9. **Data Architect**: Designs and implements data governance frameworks, including data warehousing, data lakes, and master data management.
10. **Auditor**: Performs regular audits to ensure data governance policies are being followed and data is being managed according to standards.
11. **Data Governance Board Member**: Represents various stakeholders, such as business units, IT, or external partners, to ensure data governance policies are aligned with organizational goals.
12. **Metadata Curator**: Responsible for managing and maintaining metadata, including data dictionaries, data catalogs, and data lineage.
13. **Data Standards Manager**: Develops and maintains data standards, including data definitions, data formats, and data exchange protocols.
14. **Data Governance Policy Writer**: Creates and maintains data governance policies, including data access, data quality, and data security policies.
15. **Data Training and Awareness Officer**: Develops and delivers training programs to educate stakeholders about data governance policies and best practices.

These roles can vary depending on the organization's size, industry, and data governance maturity. Some organizations may have a single person playing multiple roles, while others may have a dedicated team for each role.
