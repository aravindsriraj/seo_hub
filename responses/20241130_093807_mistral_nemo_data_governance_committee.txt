A Data Governance Committee is a group of stakeholders responsible for overseeing the management, availability, usability, integrity, and security of data in an organization. Here are some key roles and responsibilities of a Data Governance Committee:

1. **Data Governance Leadership**: Provide strategic direction and leadership for data governance programs.

2. **Data Policies and Standards**: Develop, maintain, and communicate data policies, standards, and procedures to ensure they align with business needs and regulatory requirements.

3. **Data Quality and Integrity**: Establish and monitor data quality initiatives to ensure data accuracy, completeness, timeliness, and consistency.

4. **Data Access and Security**: Define and oversee data access controls, security measures, and data privacy to protect sensitive information.

5. **Data Metadata Management**: Maintain and manage metadata to facilitate data discovery, understanding, and usage.

6. **Data Documentation**: Ensure that data documentation is up-to-date, accurate, and accessible.

7. **Data Usage and Compliance**: Monitor and ensure compliance with data usage policies, laws, and regulations, such as GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, etc.

8. **Data Education and Awareness**: Promote data literacy, awareness, and best practices across the organization.

9. **Data Project Oversight**: Oversee data-related projects to ensure they follow data governance principles and contribute to the organization's data strategy.

10. **Data Strategy Alignment**: Ensure that data governance efforts align with the organization's overall strategy and objectives.

The committee should consist of representatives from various departments to ensure a holistic approach to data governance, such as:

- IT/IS/Data Management
- Business Lines/Units
- Risk and Compliance
- Legal/General Counsel
- Data Stewards
- Data Analysts/Scientists
- Finance
- Human Resources
- External Auditors/Regulators (as required)

The committee should be led by a Chief Data Officer (CDO) or another senior executive who has the authority to drive data governance initiatives and ensure they have the necessary resources and support.
